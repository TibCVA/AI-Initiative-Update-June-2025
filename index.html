Proposal of AI Governance Principles

Ensuring responsible and secure use of generative AI across all client engagements

An official guideline needs to be communicated to everyone to ensure good practices and accountability towards our clients. Direct managers should be in charge of checking for wrong AI usages within their projects.

The use of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) tools is subject to a charter of best practices communicated to all CVA employees.

Proposed governance principles:

1
Principle 1: Compliance & Authorization
The use of generative AI tools must strictly comply with the Client's internal policies, and requires prior authorization for each use case
2
Principle 2: Information Protection
No confidential or sensitive information may be shared with LLMs (Large Language Models). Only LLMs with strict data governance policies‚Äîguaranteeing no user data is used for training‚Äîare authorized
3
Principle 3: Consultant Training
All consultants must complete dedicated training on responsible AI usage, covering risks associated with generative AI and best practices for maintaining information confidentiality
